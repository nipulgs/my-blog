- W.c.b. Sayers
+ Wyndham Hulme
- J D Brown
- H. E. bliss

- The term "Literary Warrant" for book classification was introduced by E. Wyndham Hulme (E.W. Hulme) in 1911. As a librarian at the Patent Office Library in London, Hulme proposed that classes and subjects in a classification scheme should be derived from the existing literature, not from a predetermined philosophical order.
